Professor James Okafor directs the Functional Genomics Platform at the Wellcome Sanger Institute, where his group develops high-throughput CRISPR screening methodologies to dissect gene function at scale. His lab is responsible for some of the largest genome-wide perturbation screens performed in primary human cells to date.

Originally trained as a biochemist at the University of Lagos, he completed his doctoral work at the Broad Institute under the mentorship of Feng Zhang, before establishing his independent group at Sanger in 2019. His research bridges wet-lab high-throughput biology with sophisticated statistical frameworks to identify causal relationships between genotype and phenotype in complex cell systems.

A key focus of his current program is the integration of pooled CRISPR screens with single-cell readouts (Perturb-seq) to enable simultaneous profiling of transcriptomic responses to thousands of genetic perturbations in a single experiment — scaling the throughput of functional genomics by two orders of magnitude.
