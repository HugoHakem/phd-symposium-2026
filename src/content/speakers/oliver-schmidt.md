Professor Oliver Schmidt is a pioneer in spatial genomics, having co-developed several technologies that enable simultaneous measurement of gene expression, chromatin accessibility, and protein abundance while preserving the spatial context of cells within tissues. His work bridges technology development with fundamental questions about how tissue architecture governs cell behavior.

He trained at the University of Heidelberg and completed his PhD in molecular biology at EMBL before establishing his independent research group at the Wellcome Sanger Institute. His early career work on multiplexed FISH-based methods laid groundwork for today's spatial transcriptomics revolution.

His current group focuses on scaling spatial multi-omics to whole organs and eventually whole organisms. Recent work from his lab has produced the first spatially-resolved multi-omic atlas of the human heart at single-cell resolution, revealing how cell-state transitions are governed by both intrinsic programs and extrinsic signals from the microenvironment. He is a co-investigator on the Human Cell Atlas spatial working group.
