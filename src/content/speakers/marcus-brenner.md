Dr. Marcus Brenner leads the Computational Structural Proteomics group at ETH Zurich's Department of Computational Science. His group sits at the interface of deep learning, structural biology, and systems biology, developing graph-based machine learning models that predict and model protein–protein interactions at the scale of entire proteomes.

He completed his bachelor's and master's degrees in mathematics and computer science at TU Munich, followed by his PhD at the University of Washington in David Baker's group, where he worked on Rosetta-based protein design. His postdoctoral work at DeepMind contributed to early versions of AlphaFold-Multimer before he joined ETH Zurich.

His current research develops next-generation interactome prediction models that integrate structural predictions, co-evolution signals, and proteomics data to build comprehensive, dynamic protein interaction networks. A key challenge his group tackles is scaling these methods to handle the entire proteome of model organisms simultaneously, enabling the first systems-level view of how the interactome changes in response to perturbations.
