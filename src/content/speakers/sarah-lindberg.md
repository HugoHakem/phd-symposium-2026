Dr. Sarah Lindberg leads the Proteome Dynamics group at the Sir William Dunn School of Pathology, University of Oxford. Her research pushes the boundaries of mass spectrometry-based proteomics, developing sample preparation, data acquisition, and computational methods that enable deep protein quantification from vanishingly small samples — down to single cells.

She trained as a biochemist at Uppsala University in Sweden and completed her PhD at the Max Planck Institute of Biochemistry with Matthias Mann, one of the field's founding figures. Her postdoctoral work at the ETH Zurich focused on developing data-independent acquisition methods for ultra-high sensitivity proteomics.

Her group's recent development of nanoscale proteomics workflows capable of quantifying over 5,000 proteins from single cells — without amplification artifacts — has opened the door to studying protein-level cell heterogeneity for the first time. This work complements single-cell transcriptomics and reveals a new layer of biological complexity: post-transcriptional regulation operating at the single-cell level.
