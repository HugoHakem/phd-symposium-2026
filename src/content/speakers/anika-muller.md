Dr. Anika Müller leads the Structural Cell Biology unit at EMBL Heidelberg, where her group pioneers the use of cryo-electron tomography combined with subtomogram averaging to resolve macromolecular assemblies in their native cellular context. Her group's recent breakthrough in resolving the human nuclear pore complex at sub-4Å resolution in intact nuclei has redefined our understanding of nucleocytoplasmic transport.

Prior to EMBL, she completed her postdoctoral work at the MRC Laboratory of Molecular Biology in Cambridge, where she developed novel sample preparation methods that significantly improved the resolution achievable by cryo-ET. She received her PhD from the University of Zurich under Prof. Andreas Plückthun.

Her lab currently focuses on scaling cryo-ET workflows using machine learning for automated particle picking and classification, enabling structural studies of hundreds of unique macromolecular complexes per experiment — transforming structural biology from a one-molecule-at-a-time discipline to a system-scale science.
