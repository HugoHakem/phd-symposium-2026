Professor Elena Vasquez holds the Chair in Systems Medicine at the Karolinska Institutet's Department of Medical Biochemistry and Biophysics. Her research applies network science, machine learning, and multi-omics integration to understand how genetic variation propagates through molecular networks to cause complex diseases.

Born in Barcelona, she trained in physics and completed her PhD in biophysics at the University of Barcelona before a decade-long journey through postdoctoral positions at Harvard Medical School and the Max Planck Institute for Molecular Genetics. She established her independent group at Karolinska in 2016.

Her landmark 2023 paper in Nature Medicine introduced a framework for building patient-specific disease networks from multi-omic data that can predict drug response with unprecedented accuracy. Her current work uses population-scale biobank data — millions of individuals with linked genomic, proteomic, and clinical records — to build the first dynamic, population-level disease network atlas.
