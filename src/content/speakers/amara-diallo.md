Dr. Amara Diallo leads the Spatial Neuroscience group at the Max Planck Institute of Biochemistry in Martinsried. Her group uses cutting-edge spatial transcriptomics and imaging technologies to study how the human brain develops — from the earliest stages of neural tube formation through cortical folding — in both model systems and human tissue.

Originally from Dakar, Senegal, she studied medicine and neuroscience at Université Paris Descartes before completing her PhD at the Pasteur Institute. Her postdoctoral work in Karl Deisseroth's group at Stanford on spatiotemporal gene expression analysis earned her several prestigious fellowships.

Her lab's recent work generating the first spatially-resolved single-cell atlas of human fetal brain development spanning 9 to 25 weeks of gestation has provided unprecedented insight into the origins of neurodevelopmental disorders. She uses cerebral organoids as scalable models to dissect the molecular programs that go awry in conditions such as autism spectrum disorder and microcephaly — combining high-throughput perturbation with spatial readouts.
