Dr. Priya Sharma directs the AI for Cancer Biology division at DKFZ in Heidelberg, where her interdisciplinary team applies large-scale machine learning to accelerate cancer drug discovery. Her research focuses on building foundation models — large, pre-trained neural networks — that learn universal representations of cancer cell states from hundreds of thousands of transcriptomic profiles.

Trained in biomedical engineering at IIT Bombay and holding a PhD in machine learning from the University of Toronto (under Geoffrey Hinton's lab alumni), she joined DKFZ in 2021 after a stint at Google DeepMind's health team. She was listed in MIT Technology Review's Innovators Under 35 in 2022.

Her group's OncoGPT-2 model, trained on over 500,000 single-cell profiles from cancer patient biopsies, can predict drug sensitivity with high accuracy and identify novel therapeutic vulnerabilities. Her work exemplifies the convergence of AI and biology — using the scale of deep learning to extract patterns invisible to conventional analysis.
