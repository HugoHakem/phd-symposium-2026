Dr. Li Wei's research group at the Broad Institute is at the forefront of building reference single-cell atlases at planetary scale. Her lab develops computational and experimental frameworks for integrating single-cell RNA sequencing data across species, tissues, developmental stages, and disease states to build a universal reference of cell identity.

She trained in computational biology at Peking University before completing her PhD at Stanford University, followed by postdoctoral work in Aviv Regev's lab at the Broad. Her group's work on cross-dataset integration algorithms, particularly the Harmony and scATAC-BERT methods, has been adopted as a standard in the field with over 15,000 citations combined.

Current projects in her lab focus on the Human Cell Atlas initiative, developing methods to scale single-cell data analysis to hundreds of millions of cells while preserving biological signal and correcting for technical batch effects, enabling the discovery of rare cell populations and disease-specific transcriptional programs.
